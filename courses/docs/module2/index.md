# Module 2 · AI That Tunes the Senses

**Multimodal AI, Sound, Image, Atmosphere — Weeks 7–11 · Led by [Youngjun Choi](https://youngjuene.github.io/)**

How do machines record, model, and recompose what we see and hear? This module
examines the gap between human sensory experience and AI's way of processing
data — and treats that gap as creative material.

## Guiding Questions

1. What aesthetic symptoms reveal the differences between human sensory
   experience and AI's way of processing data?
2. When sensory information is recorded as data and converted into a model's
   internal representations, what is preserved and what is lost?
3. If this transformation were composed differently, would a sensory
   experience unique to AI mediation become possible?

## Assignment

Produce a creative work (individual or team) that stages the transformation
between sensory experience and machine representation — a sonification, a
visualization, a cross-modal translation, an interactive system, or a critical
artifact.

- **Proposal** — presented in class in Week 8 (Oct 22): concept, references,
  technical plan, division of roles.
- **Final work** — presented and critiqued in Week 11 (Nov 12); shown again at
  the final exhibition (Week 16, Dec 17).
- **Sharing** — every proposal and final work is posted to the
  [Assignment Dashboard](dashboard.md); feedback happens in class during the
  critique sessions.
