## Overview

A studio course in which students carry out collaborative creation centered on
practice, using the technologies and AI tools employed in creative work.
Together we explore the full arc of creation — from discovering and defining
problems, through developing ideas, planning and production, to critique and
presentation.

The course is a convergent curriculum that embraces **Technical Creators** who
design and realize experiences with new media and technologies, **Critical
Interpreters** who critically read the aesthetics and social meaning of works,
and **Creative Technicians** who build the technical foundations that make
creation possible. Rather than treating AI as a mere production tool, we
critically examine how human senses and creativity, interpretation and
judgment, and modes of collaboration and authorship are being transformed and
reconfigured in the age of AI — and apply these reflections to actual creative
processes. We also experiment with AI-based planning, analysis, and
interaction, thinking through the relationship between technology and humans
in contemporary aesthetic, social, and philosophical contexts.

Through individual and team projects, guest lectures, and an exhibition,
students produce and share AI-based outcomes, documenting and presenting both
process and result. The course is linked with *Critical AI: Critique and
Theory*, offered in the spring semester.

## What is Creative AI? — Guiding Questions

### Module 1 · AI That Designs Space: Architecture, Environment, City

1. In the AI era, what forms of efficiency and creativity can architectural
   design, construction, maintenance, and cities achieve? (Weeks 2–4)
2. How does AI reconfigure the designer's decision-making in architectural
   design, expanding and deepening the scope of thought?
3. In what ways do spaces designed with AI participation differ from those
   without it, and what are their strengths and weaknesses?

### Module 2 · AI That Tunes the Senses: Multimodal AI, Sound, Image, Atmosphere

!!! info "Led by [Youngjun Choi](https://youngjuene.github.io/) — see the [module page](module2/index.md) for sessions and the [assignment dashboard](module2/dashboard.md)"

1. What aesthetic symptoms reveal the differences between human sensory
   experience and AI's way of processing data?
2. When sensory information is recorded as data and converted into a model's
   internal representations, what is preserved and what is lost?
3. If this transformation were composed differently, would a sensory
   experience unique to AI mediation become possible?

### Module 3 · Brain and Creation: BCI, Neuroaesthetics, Human–Machine Collaboration

1. How can brain activity be interpreted and expressed — what role does AI
   play in this process, and how far can its possibilities extend?
2. Can the brain become a new interface — how does AI turn human states into
   direct input, and how open are those possibilities?
3. How does creation mediated by the brain differ from creation that is not?
